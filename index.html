A CULINARY ADVENTURE IN MEXICO CITY

By: Violetta Torres-Barón

Sopes in Condesa


Our first night in Mexico City we ordered Sopes, a traditional fried corn cake originating in the central and southern region of the country. These minature ground maize cakes are typically served as a vessel with toppings, like chicken or meat.

Chapulines in El Centro

Seasoned, deep-fried grasshoppers are a common snack across the city. I won't deny that it took me a few days to finally try these critters but I'm happy I did because they're crunchy and delicious.


Street Food: Tacos Al Pastor

Originally brought by the Lebanese immigrants to Mexico in the 19th century, Al pastor is spit-roasted pork visible on almost every street corner. It's one of Mexico City's signature dishes.


Churrrrros!

One afternoon after walking what felt like a half-marathon, we came to a locally beloved churro café established in 1935. Churros are common not only in Mexico but around Latin America, and typically paired with creamy Hot Chocolate as the ideal dessert.
